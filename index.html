Hints & Tips for Pet Owners

Few useful hints & tips for pet owners

  • No medicine to be given to the pet without prior consultation as certain human medicines are contraindicated.
  • Salt and Sugar in moderate quantities can be added to the diet of a dog. Not feeding sugar and salt is a myth.
  • After coming back from a walk, a dog should be checked at all paws for the presence of any foreign body.
  • Do not buy or acquire a pup of less than 5 weeks of age.
  • Do not keep a newly acquired pup on the floor; keep him/her on bedding in a box or basket.
  • Pup should be combed & brushed every day. Pet talc/baby powder or coat-care deodorant may be used for cleanliness & fragrance. Frequent bathing is not recommended.
  • Always feed the pup with lukewarm fresh feed each time.
  • Never leave your dog in a closed car with windows rolled up — risk of heat stroke.
  • Pups normally do not drink water, so do not force water into the pup's feed.
  • In young pups, diarrhoea or vomiting needs immediate veterinary attention.
  • Minced meat can be introduced to pups at 3 months of age.
  • Dog's normal body temperature is between 101°F – 102°F.
  • Dogs should be trained from an early age for toileting; move him to a regular spot after every meal.
  • Call your dog by name each time so that it responds and recognises quickly.
  • A dog is mature for breeding at 1–1.5 years. Do not mate a bitch on her 1st heat cycle.
  • A bitch's heat lasts about 21 days; bleeding phase is 5–10 days.
  • Successful mating window: 8–12 days for small breeds, 10–14 for large breeds after onset of bleeding.
  • Gestation (pregnancy) in bitches lasts 58–62 days.
  • Chaining dogs continuously makes them furious; avoid chaining at all times.
  • All dogs need exercise, good food, hygienic living conditions, and love & affection.
  • Professional training should only start after 4 months of age.

Common ailments

At the Dr. Anand's Pets Clinic, we aim to educate the pet owners to be able to take care of their pets more efficiently and at the same time, help them identify a few common ailments in their pets which might call for veterinary help before it's too late.

Basically the body of a dog / cat functions exactly like the human body but it lacks a lot of the advanced stuff in the human system. The basics of cleanliness, hygiene, and quality of food stand true for your pets as much as they are for us humans.

Symptoms of worms in dogs

  • Appears weak and restless
  • Appetite changes, may eat either more or less
  • May show signs of constipation or Diarrhoea
  • Coat may be rough and poor
  • Eyes give watery white thick discharge
  • Looks anaemic
  • Eats rubbish and feels like eating own stool, grass, paper, eating from dustbins etc.

Symptoms of a sick dog

  • Loses his pep (activeness)
  • Coat is dull and loses shine
  • Sluggish movement
  • Dry nose and discharge from nose
  • Eyes running & discharge from eyes; eyes could be red or pale
  • Noticeable difference in accepting food
  • May have loose motion, constipation or vomiting
  • Unusual tiredness and limping etc.
  • Shaking of head & twitching of ears
  • Scratching often
  • Lumps and swelling on body
  • Variation in body temperature and respiration

Regular checks required for your dogs & anal glands cleaning

At the Dr. Anand's Pets Clinic, we provide numerous services to ensure that pet owners have an easy life by finding all they need for their pet care under one roof. Many pet owners like to maintain the health and fitness of their pet to the best of standards for various reasons.

A dog's anal glands are the two small sacs bracketing from the anus. If you notice that your dog is continually licking his rectal area or dragging this area across the ground, it may indicate that the anal glands of the dog are full or infected. Make sure to consult your veterinarian about this.

Usually, the way to deal with this problem is by regularly emptying out or expressing the anal glands. When beginning this procedure, often times you will be able to notice a bulging and this indicates that the anal glands are obstructed. When the anal glands are emptied out correctly this bulging usually goes away.

Next, wash out the black, brown, or gray substance that comes out with shampoo in order to get rid of the unpleasant odor. If you see that the dog seems to be in a lot of pain, or if the substance that comes out of the dog's anal glands is green, it could mean that there is an infection so make sure to get hold of your veterinarian.
