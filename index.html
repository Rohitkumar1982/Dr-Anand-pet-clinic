Hints & Tips for Pet Owners

No medicine should be given to your pet without prior consultation with a veterinarian. Certain human medicines can be dangerous or contraindicated for animals.

Salt and sugar in moderate quantities can be added to your dog's diet. It is a myth that dogs should not be fed any sugar or salt.

After coming back from a walk, always check all paws for the presence of any foreign body, such as thorns, seeds, or ticks.

Never leave your dog in a closed car with windows rolled up. Dogs can't tolerate the greenhouse effect and heat inside the vehicle, which can cause heat stroke.

Dogs are generally considered mature for breeding at around 1 to 1.5 years of age. Female dogs (bitches) should not be mated on their first heat cycle.
